	<p>Gossip Girl is an American teen drama television series 
	based on the book</br> series of the same name written by Cecily 
	von Ziegesar.</br> The series was created by Josh Schwartz and 
	Stephanie Savage,</br> and premiered on The CW on September 19, 2007. </br>
	Narrated by the omniscient blogger "Gossip Girl", voiced by Kristen 
	Bell,</br> the series revolves around the lives of privileged young adults</br>
	on Manhattan's Upper East Side in New York City. </p>
	
	<p>The series begins with the return of Upper East Side "It" girl,</br>
	Serena van der Woodsen (Blake Lively)</br> from a mysterious stay at a boarding school
	in Cornwall, Connecticut.</br> Blair Waldorf (Leighton Meester), whom creators describe
	as the </br>queen at the center of their chess game, is a longtime friend and </br>
	occasional rival of Serena's, and the Queen Bee of Constance Billard School's social scene.</br>
	The story also follows Chuck Bass (Ed Westwick), the bad boy of the Upper East Side;</br>
	"golden boy" Nate Archibald (Chace Crawford), Chuck's best friend and Blair's boyfriend</br> for many years.
	However, their relationship had been rocky ever since Serena left for boarding school.</br>
	Other characters of the turbulent Manhattan scene: Dan Humphrey (Penn Badgley),</br>
	Dan's best friend Vanessa Abrams (Jessica Szohr),</br> Dan's sister, Jenny Humphrey (Taylor Momsen),</br>
	and con artist Ivy Dickens (Kaylee DeFer).</p>
	
	<p>The show has received numerous award nominations,</br>
	winning 18 Teen Choice Awards.</br> The CW officially renewed 
	Gossip Girl</br> for a sixth and final season on May 11, 2012.</br>
	The final season, consisting of 10 episodes,</br> premiered on 
	October 8, 2012, and <b>will end in late December.</b></p>
